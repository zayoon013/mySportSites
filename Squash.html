üöÄ Amazing Squash Fun Facts

Prison Origins

Squash was invented around 1830 at Harrow School in England when students discovered hitting a punctured racquets ball against a wall was more fun!

Olympic Bid

Despite multiple attempts, squash has never been included in the Olympics, though it came close for the 2012 and 2016 games!

Lightning Fast

The squash ball can reach speeds of up to 176 mph (283 km/h) when hit by professional players!

Calorie Crusher

Squash burns more calories per minute than almost any other sport - up to 517 calories in 30 minutes!

Glass Courts

Modern tournament courts often use glass walls to allow spectators to watch from all angles, creating an incredible viewing experience!

Global Reach

Squash is played in over 185 countries worldwide, making it one of the most internationally popular racquet sports!

Court Dimensions

  • Length: 32 feet (9.75m)
  • Width: 21 feet (6.4m)
  • Height: 15 feet (4.57m)
  • Playing surface: Wood or synthetic

Court Lines

  • Service line: 1.83m from back wall
  • Short line: Middle of the court
  • Tin: 48cm high front wall metal
  • Out line: 4.57m high on front wall

üí° Pro Squash Tips

  • Master the T: Always return to the center of the court (the T) after each shot to maintain court control.
  • Watch the Ball: Keep your eyes on the ball at all times, especially when it's coming off the back wall.
  • Use the Walls: Learn to use all four walls effectively - side walls, back wall, and front wall angles.
  • Vary Your Shots: Mix up hard drives, soft drops, and deceptive boasts to keep your opponent guessing.
  • Fitness is Key: Squash demands exceptional cardiovascular fitness and quick reflexes.
  • Racquet Control: Keep your racquet up and ready between shots for quicker reaction times.
  • Ball Temperature: Warm up the ball properly before play - cold balls don't bounce well.
  • Court Positioning: Stay behind your opponent when they're hitting to avoid interference.
