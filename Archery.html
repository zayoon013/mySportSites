Ancient Origins

Archery dates back over 20,000 years! The oldest known arrowheads were found in Africa and are approximately 64,000 years old.

Olympic Legacy

Archery was one of the sports in the first modern Olympics in 1900 and has been a continuous Olympic sport since 1972!

Robin Hood's Shot

The legendary "Robin Hood" shot (splitting an arrow already in the target) is actually possible and happens in competitions!

üèπ Bow Types

Recurve Bow

  • Olympic standard bow type
  • Curved tips for extra power
  • Can be taken apart for transport
  • Great for beginners and experts

Compound Bow

  • Uses cams and pulleys for leverage
  • More powerful and accurate
  • Popular for hunting
  • Requires less strength to hold at full draw

Longbow

  • Traditional English war bow
  • Simple D-shaped design
  • Requires significant strength
  • Historical and traditional appeal

üí° Pro Archery Tips

  • Consistent Stance: Develop a repeatable stance with feet shoulder-width apart and body perpendicular to the target.
  • Anchor Point: Find a consistent anchor point where you draw the string to the same spot every time.
  • Sight Picture: Align your sight pin with the target consistently for each shot.
  • Follow Through: Keep your bow arm steady and release hand moving straight back after the shot.
  • Breathing Control: Take a deep breath, exhale halfway, then hold while aiming and shooting.
  • Mental Focus: Develop a pre-shot routine to maintain consistency and concentration.
  • Practice Regularly: Consistent practice is more valuable than occasional long sessions.
  • Proper Form: Focus on form over power - accuracy comes from consistency, not strength.

üéØ Archery Scoring

Standard target has 10 rings with gold center worth 9-10 points.
