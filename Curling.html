ü•å Amazing Curling Fun Facts

Scottish Origins

Curling originated in Scotland in the 16th century and was originally played on frozen ponds with stones from the bottom of rivers!

The Roaring Game

Curling is called "The Roaring Game" because of the sound the granite stones make as they travel across the ice!

Olympic Sport

Curling became an official Olympic sport in 1998 and has been one of the most watched winter Olympic events!

Granite Stones

All curling stones are made from granite quarried from one island in Scotland - Ailsa Craig - and each stone weighs 44 pounds!

Perfect Ice

Curling ice is pebbled with tiny water droplets that freeze to create the perfect surface for stones to curl!

Curling Brooms

Sweeping can increase a stone's distance by up to 15 feet and reduce curl by up to 6 feet!

üí° Pro Curling Tips

  • Perfect Your Delivery: Maintain consistent form with smooth, controlled release and proper follow-through.
  • Ice Reading: Learn to read the ice conditions and adjust your strategy accordingly throughout the game.
  • Sweeping Communication: Clear communication between skip and sweepers is crucial for stone placement.
  • Weight Control: Master different stone weights from draw weight to takeout weight for various shots.
  • Mental Strategy: Think several shots ahead and consider both offensive and defensive options.
  • Team Chemistry: Build trust and communication with your teammates for better performance.
  • Practice Fundamentals: Regular practice of basic shots builds muscle memory and consistency.
  • Fitness and Flexibility: Maintain good physical condition for stamina during long games.

ü•å Curling Shots

Draw Shot

Gentle shot that comes to rest in the house.

Takeout

Forceful shot designed to remove opponent stones.

Guard

Stone placed in front of the house to protect other stones.

Freeze

Shot that comes to rest touching another stone.

Raise

Shot that hits your own stone to move it forward.

Split

Shot that hits two stones and removes both.
